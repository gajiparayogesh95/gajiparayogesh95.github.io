I've been studying value investing seriously for several years now — reading annual reports, building valuation models, tracking businesses I find interesting, and making my share of mistakes. For most of that time, my thinking happened in private: in spreadsheets, in notebooks, in the margins of 10-Ks.

This blog is an attempt to change that.

## Why Write Publicly?

There are three reasons.

**Clarity of thinking.** Writing forces precision. It's easy to believe you understand a business until you try to explain it to someone else. The act of writing a thesis exposes the gaps in your reasoning that a spreadsheet happily hides.

**Accountability.** Publishing investment ideas creates a record. You can't quietly forget the mistakes or pretend the misses never happened. That kind of accountability is uncomfortable but valuable.

**The community.** Some of the best investing insights I've encountered came from reading other people's public analysis. If this blog adds even a small amount of value to someone else's process, that's worthwhile.

## What You'll Find Here

The blog is organized into a few distinct sections:

**Deep Dives** are the core of what I do. These are long-form analyses of individual businesses: what they do, how they earn their returns, what the competitive position looks like, and what the business might be worth. These take weeks to research and should be treated as a starting point for your own thinking — not a buy signal.

**Market Commentary** is for shorter, more timely posts: thoughts on valuation in a particular sector, observations about market behavior, or reflections on a recent development. Less structured than deep dives, but hopefully still useful.

**Framework** posts (like this one) explore investing concepts and mental models — things like how I think about competitive advantage, how to read a financial statement, or how I approach position sizing.

The **Philosophy**, **Watchlist**, and **Resources** pages are static: they document my investment principles, the companies I'm currently tracking, and the books and tools I find most useful.

## What This Blog Is Not

A few things this is not:

- **Financial advice.** Everything here is for educational purposes. I am not a licensed financial advisor, and nothing I write should be taken as a recommendation to buy or sell any security. Do your own research.

- **A trading service.** I have no interest in generating returns through information asymmetry or by moving small-cap prices. If anything I write moves a stock, something has gone wrong.

- **A prediction machine.** I am not going to tell you what the market will do next quarter. Nobody reliably knows that, and pretending otherwise is a service to no one.

## A Note on Mistakes

I will be wrong. Some of my analyses will miss something important. Some of my value estimates will be too optimistic. Some businesses I describe as having durable moats will lose them.

I plan to document those failures openly, because the honest accounting of what went wrong is often more instructive than the post-hoc rationalisation of what went right.
