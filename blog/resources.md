## Books

These are the books I return to most often. If you're new to value investing, read them roughly in this order.

<div class="resources-grid">
  <div class="resource-card">
    <div class="resource-type">Book — Foundation</div>
    <h3>The Intelligent Investor</h3>
    <p>Benjamin Graham. The definitive text on margin of safety, Mr. Market, and the difference between investing and speculation.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="resource-card">
    <div class="resource-type">Book — Foundation</div>
    <h3>Security Analysis</h3>
    <p>Graham &amp; Dodd. Dense and technical, but the original framework for analysing financial statements and estimating intrinsic value.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="resource-card">
    <div class="resource-type">Book — Essential</div>
    <h3>Poor Charlie's Almanack</h3>
    <p>Charlie Munger's collected talks. Mental models, multidisciplinary thinking, and the best framework for understanding competitive advantages.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="resource-card">
    <div class="resource-type">Book — Essential</div>
    <h3>The Essays of Warren Buffett</h3>
    <p>Curated Berkshire letters by Lawrence Cunningham. Better than reading all the letters individually — organised thematically.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="resource-card">
    <div class="resource-type">Book — Competitive Advantage</div>
    <h3>Competition Demystified</h3>
    <p>Bruce Greenwald. Practical framework for identifying moats using microeconomics. Clearer than Porter for investment purposes.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="resource-card">
    <div class="resource-type">Book — Valuation</div>
    <h3>Valuation (McKinsey)</h3>
    <p>Koller, Goedhart &amp; Wessels. The most rigorous treatment of DCF, ROIC, and what actually drives enterprise value.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="resource-card">
    <div class="resource-type">Book — Psychology</div>
    <h3>Thinking, Fast and Slow</h3>
    <p>Kahneman. Understanding your own biases is as important as any financial framework. Required reading for any investor.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="resource-card">
    <div class="resource-type">Book — Case Studies</div>
    <h3>The Outsiders</h3>
    <p>William Thorndike. Eight CEOs who compounded capital at extraordinary rates — a masterclass in rational capital allocation.</p>
  </div>
</div>

## Research Tools

<div class="resources-grid">
  <div class="resource-card">
    <div class="resource-type">Primary Source</div>
    <h3>SEC EDGAR</h3>
    <p>Annual reports (10-K), quarterly filings (10-Q), proxy statements (DEF 14A). Always go to the primary source.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="resource-card">
    <div class="resource-type">Screener</div>
    <h3>Stratosphere / Tikr</h3>
    <p>Clean financial data, historical ROIC, and earnings transcripts. Good for building a shortlist before deep research.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="resource-card">
    <div class="resource-type">Screener</div>
    <h3>Finviz</h3>
    <p>Fast quantitative screening by P/E, P/FCF, ROIC, debt ratios. Useful for top-of-funnel filtering.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="resource-card">
    <div class="resource-type">Data</div>
    <h3>Macrotrends</h3>
    <p>Long historical series for revenue, margins, ROIC, and cash flow. Good for seeing how a business behaves across cycles.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="resource-card">
    <div class="resource-type">Industry Research</div>
    <h3>IBISWorld / Statista</h3>
    <p>Industry structure, market size, and competitive landscape. Useful context for the qualitative part of any analysis.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="resource-card">
    <div class="resource-type">Transcripts</div>
    <h3>Seeking Alpha / Rev.com</h3>
    <p>Earnings call transcripts. Reading how management talks about their business over multiple years is revealing.</p>
  </div>
</div>

## Letters and Writing Worth Reading

- **Berkshire Hathaway Annual Letters** (1965–present) — Buffett's thinking in full, free on Berkshire's website
- **Markel Annual Letters** — Tom Gayner's thoughtful writing on insurance and investment
- **Ensemble Capital Blog** — High-quality writing on competitive advantage and business quality
- **Base Hit Investing (John Huber)** — Practical value investing with emphasis on ROIC and reinvestment
